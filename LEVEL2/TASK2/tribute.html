About Sandeep Unnikrishnan

Sandeep Unnikrishnan was a brave and dedicated officer in the Indian Army. He was born on March 15, 1977, in Bangalore, India. He always dreamt of serving his nation and joined the army at a young age with a strong sense of duty and patriotism.


Sandeep excelled in his military training and quickly rose through the ranks, earning the respect of his peers and superiors. He was known for his leadership skills, unwavering courage, and selflessness, which would be put to the ultimate test during the 2008 Mumbai attacks.
Sandeep Unnikrishnan was an Indian Army officer, born on March 15, 1977, in Bangalore, India. He was commissioned as a commissioned officer in the Indian Army and served in the elite special forces unit, the National Security Guard (NSG). He was part of the NSG's 51 Special Action Group (51 SAG), which is a highly trained unit for counter-terrorism and hostage rescue operations.
During the 2008 Mumbai attacks, when multiple locations in Mumbai were targeted by terrorists, Sandeep Unnikrishnan displayed exceptional bravery and leadership. He was leading a team of commandos in a rescue operation at the Taj Mahal Palace Hotel, where hostages were held by terrorists. Despite being critically injured, he continued to engage the terrorists to ensure the safety of the hostages. Sandeep made the ultimate sacrifice for the nation during the operation, and he was posthumously awarded the Ashoka Chakra, India's highest peacetime gallantry award, for his bravery.
